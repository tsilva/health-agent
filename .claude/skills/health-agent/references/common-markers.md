## Age-Specific Range Adjustments

### Pediatric (under 18)
- Glucose: Lower normal range (60-100 mg/dL)
- ALP: Higher normal due to bone growth (up to 500 U/L in adolescents)
- Hemoglobin: Varies significantly by age group

### Geriatric (65+)
- eGFR: Accept slightly lower values as normal age-related decline
- TSH: Upper limit may be higher (up to 5-6 mIU/L acceptable)
- Vitamin D: 30+ ng/mL recommended vs 20+ for younger adults

## Gender-Specific Range Adjustments

### Males
| Marker | Typical Range |
|--------|--------------|
| Hemoglobin | 13.5-17.5 g/dL |
| Hematocrit | 38.8-50% |
| Creatinine | 0.7-1.3 mg/dL |
| Ferritin | 24-336 ng/mL |
| Testosterone | 270-1070 ng/dL |

### Females
| Marker | Typical Range |
|--------|--------------|
| Hemoglobin | 12.0-16.0 g/dL |
| Hematocrit | 34.9-44.5% |
| Creatinine | 0.6-1.1 mg/dL |
| Ferritin | 11-307 ng/mL |
| Testosterone | 15-70 ng/dL |

### Females - Pregnancy Adjustments
- Hemoglobin: Lower threshold (11 g/dL)
- TSH: First trimester 0.1-2.5 mIU/L
- eGFR: Increased during pregnancy
- Glucose: Stricter thresholds for gestational diabetes screening
